1. The Basis
In order to minimize drama and maximize success the following criteria should be met by a loot-system:
  • Fairness
  • Reliability
  • Simplicity
  • Flexibility
  • Transparency
What should also be considered is the fact that it shouldn't in any way, shape nor form be able to be abused by neither a single individual nor a group of people. Here is a list of commonly used loot-systems, how they work and what their strengths and weaknesses are:
Loot-council
A select group of people, generally officers and the GM, decide who get what item based solely on their opinions. This system CAN be extremely fair, reliable and flexible but runs a high risk of being abused by previously mentioned people. And even if it is not abused the transparency will always be an issue due to the simple fact that individual members have no way of discerning where they currently stand as far as loot goes. Another issue is the simplicity, it demands A LOT of work in order to be properly executed.
Suicide Kings
A system where every member is sorted in a big list where the highest person will get whichever item they so desire (within limits of course). As soon as said item is awarded they drop down to last place and have to wait until they are at, or close to, the top to receive an item again. The biggest weakness of this is the flexibility and reliability. There is also the problem with fairness where it can easily be abused without any reprimand.
DKP
Killing bosses award attending members with DKP (Dragon Killing Points) and said points are spent to either buy or bid for items that drop. Transparency and fairness is usually not a problem with DKP however it is not always so flexible and reliable. It can unfortunately easily be abused by simply hoarding DKP. Where it falls other than that is generally that it has some side-effects such as inflation/deflation and in either of those cases it makes the system unfair either for or against new members that join in the middle of a raid-tier.
FFA
Not an alternative given the criteria listed above.
Need/Greed
Simply rolling on items that drop that are appropriate for class/spec. Now this system is undoubtedly simple, reliable and transparent but it falls extremely short in flexibility and fairness. Leaving loot up to luck of the draw will undoubtedly lead to some extremely unhappy individuals who happen upon unlucky streaks.
EPGP
EPGP stands for Effort Points/Gear Points and is a system that is most closely related to DKP. But instead of simply using points as a currency it instead gives a value which in turn is used to decide who gets which item. It also factors in a weekly decay which has two main functions: limiting the possibility for individual players to hoard points and keeping all players within a set margin of points. The way the points are tracked can be viewed by anyone and the number of ways to earn EP can be customizable to fit any need. At first the system might seem confusing or even convoluted but it is extremely easy to maintain and control. The system itself also allows for easy handling of new members and the effect they have on an already started tier.
2. EPGP
Taking the information above into account we have decided to use EPGP as our tool to monitor the efforts of our members and distribution of items that drop in raids. We feel it meets the criterias stated above to the fullest extent and will lead to an enviroment everyone can enjoy.
2.1 How it works
As previously mentioned EP/GP stands for Effort Points / Gear Points. The way members accumulate EP can be through a multitude of ways. Such as being inside the instance when the raid-time starts, standing at the ready to jump in if needed or simply by killing bosses. GP on the other hand is awarded when choosing to take a piece of loot. Different pieces have a different value so the better the item the more GP.

The next step in the process is to simply divide your current EP with your current GP, the result of this is your priority for future drops. The higher the number the higher your priority. You then compare the priority of all members asking for a specifik drop and whoever has the highest gets said item.

Example:
An item drops that costs 60 GP. Both Alpha and Beta wants the item and both are eligable to have it. To see who gets it we have to compare their priority:
  • Alphas Effort Points (from doing various things) are 240
  • Alphas Gear Points (from taking a few items previously) are 120
  • 240 / 120 = 2
  • Thus Alphas priority is 2
  • Betas Effort Points (from doing various things) are 260
  • Betas Gear Points (from taking a few items previously) are 180
  • 260 / 180 = 1.4
  • Thus Betas priority is 1.4
  • Alphas priority is higher which means he/she gets the item. After the item is awarded Alphas new priority is (240/180=) 1.3 and that means that Beta will get the next item (as long as no one with a higher priority wants that item).
As you can tell the longer between each item you have gotten, the higher your priority will be and sooner or later yours will be higher than those you compete against.

So what happens if two people have the same priority? The system will automatically do a roll for each player choosing that they are interested in the item. These rolls can then be compared and whoever rolled highest wins the item. This will happen a lot in the beginning but the further we get the fewer times it will have to be resorted to.
3. Effort Points (EP)
The way in which we will be tracking each individual members dedication, commitment and diligence is through Effort Points. If you show up for raids, come prepared and stay the course you will undoubtedly be awarded for it. The gaining of EP is your way of making sure you will get loot, which in turn will make your character and the whole guild stronger.
3.1 When EP will be awarded
EP will be awarded to members through these means:
  • Being inside the raid and at the ready 18.30
  • Having a food-buff up when we do the first pull
  • Killing every boss
  • For every 30 minutes spent raiding
  • Staying throughout the entire raid
  • Being ready to step in if the raid is full. Ready means either logged out just outside the raid and sitting available on Discord or being online in-game and farming/idling.
4. Gear Points (GP)
The manner in which you track how “much” gear a single individual has gotten is through Gear Points. The amount of GP a specific piece costs is determined by a number of factors and this is to make sure different items cost different amounts depending on their worth. A 2 Handed Epic Sword should definitively cost more than a Rare Ring, and an Epic ring from a later Tier should cost more than an Epic Ring from a Lower one.
4.1 Formula
The formula used in order to determine an items GP is as follows:

0,483 x (2^((ilvl/26)+(rarity-4)) x slot) x 1

ILVL: Fixed depending on the stats on the item, it is an easy way to value items from different Tiers against each other. Generally all items from a raid have a similar ilvl with the exception being the last boss that tends to drop items with a higher ilvl.

Rarity: Will not be relevant on very many occasions but should absolutely be taken into account. It is only regarded by two formulas:
  • Rare: TBD
  • Epic: TBD
Slot: The last variable part of the equation is slots. It is important for different slots to cost different amounts due to the shear value. A Chestpiece is a lot more worth than Bracers for example.
  • Head: 1
  • Neck: 0.5
  • Shoulder: 0.75
  • Cloak: 0.5
  • Chest: 1
  • Wrist: 0.5
  • Hands: 0.75
  • Waist: 0.75
  • Legs: 1
  • Feet: 0.75
  • Ring: 0.5
  • Trinket: 0.75
  • 1H Weapon: 1.25
  • 2H Weapon: 2/0.5*
  • Shield: 0.5
  • Ranged: 0.5/2*
  • Wand: 0,5
  • Relic: 0.5
  • Thrown: 0.5
  • Caster OH: 0.5
*Example of exceptions, more below.

Reasoning behind weapons:

The biggest point of discussion is without a doubt the disparities between using two 1-H, a 1-H and OH or a 2H. These costs don't match up, instead they fall on increments om 1.75, 2 and 2.5. The reasoning behind this is as follows:

There is no doubt that one of the most sought out upgrades is weapons for melee. This due to the added value of not simply being used for the base stats. The DPS is paramount for melee. As a result the heavily contested 1-H will have to be tolled with quite a price. The question that might arise then is: “Why doesn't 2-H cost the same price? It has the same added effect.”. The answer is that there just isn't the same type of desire for those weapons. There is also the added effect of having two enchantments etc. As far as casters go there is a lot of simplicity in taking a Staff. While taking a MH and OH requires more effort. Thus the 0.25 difference.

This is by no means perfect but it doesn't put any one group in a too good/bad of a position.
4.2 Exceptions
Now there are a few exceptions when it comes to GP. This is to help relieve some of the problems that could arise if the system is too rigid. These exceptions are all made in order to make gearing as smooth and fair as possible while also making sure we have the best possibilities to progress.
4.2.1 Ranged/2-Handers
The first exception to general cost-rules are Ranged Weapons and 2-Handed Weapons for Melee/Hunters. For Hunters a 2-Handed Weapon will have a coefficient of 0,5 and Ranged Weapons 2,0. The opposite is true for Melee: a 2-Handed Weapon will have a coefficient of 2,0 and Ranged Weapons 0,5. But not only that, due to the imbalance this might cause Melee will automatically have a priority for 2-Handed Weapons and Hunters for Ranged. This is to make sure items that are simply used as stat-sticks won't cost too much, and Main Weapons won't cost too little.
4.2.2 Tanking
The next exception has to do with DPS-Warriors and Tanking Gear. As per usual Tanks have a priority on all Tanking items, and the Main Tank specifically. This makes sure that the people who have to be hit in the face and survive do just that; survive. But what happens when designated Tanks have the loot they need? Then said items will be handed to other Warriors free of charge. Why? Well it is quite simple, without properly geared tanks we will not succeed and getting far enough they will be required (looking at you 4-H). Thus it is one of the few positions where you really don't have an option. A Healer doesn't HAVE to take items for DPS, neither do the designated Tanks. And should our DPS have to gimp their position in order to accomodate a forced need? Not really. Thus left-over pieces of gear for tanking will be handed out.
4.2.3 Thunderfury and Atiesh
The last exception will be Thunderfury and Atiesh. Now these items will not be handed out on a whim, nor based on who just happened to be on top of the priority. These items will have to be decided before they drop. As far as Thunderfury is concerned it will undoubtedly go to the Main Tank first and to other Tanks thereafter. This is bescause of the insane threat-increase it provides for the tanks, which in turn makes life easier for everyone else. Atiesh is still to be determined.
5. Decay
EPGP has a built in tool in order to minimize the risk, or at least punish the act, of hoarding. This is done through a decay. At a fixed interval, in this case every Wednesday, 5% of every members EP and GP will be deducted. This means that everyones priority will stay the same but the overall point-difference will be decreased. The decay then also fill an important role of making sure long-time raiders still have all the reason to attend raids. Sure your EP goes down but so does your GP and thus raiding again will increase your EP while not taking loot will keep your priority high. This leads us into the next point: changing of raid-tiers. When a new tier of 40-man raiding is introduced we will have a forced decay of 40%. This is done in order to soft-reset the system but still make sure that dedicated people get a priority on loot.

7. New Members
As we progress the perfect scenario would be that we keep the exact same lineup throughout, and we will always strive to keep everyone in the guild happy, content and involved. However sometimes things happen and new members will have to be recruited. There will probably never exist a solution to loot and new members that will please everyone but hopefully our option will be a middle ground that everyone can accept. To make sure new members get a worthwhile introduction, and still make sure our long-time members don't feel bypassed, we will have a minimum requirement of EP in order to get loot. This requirement should equate to approximately 2-3 weeks of raiding. Then they are allowed to use their priority to take loot in front of others. It should be noted that receiving loot is still an option, however this is strictly for gear that should otherwise be DE’d.
