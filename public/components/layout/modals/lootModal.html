1. The Basis
In order to minimize drama, and maximize success, the following criteria should be met by a loot system:
  • Fairness
  • Reliability
  • Simplicity
  • Flexibility
  • Transparency
What should also be considered is the fact that the system shouldn't be abusable by a single individual or group of people. With that in mind we've elected to use EPGP.
2. Effort Points (EP)
The way in which we will be tracking each individual members dedication, commitment and diligence is through Effort Points. If you show up for raids, come prepared and stay the course you will undoubtedly be awarded for it. The earning of EP is your way of making sure you will get loot, which in turn will make your character (and the guild) stronger. The Raid Manager is responsible for the handling of EP. EP will be awarded to members through these means:
  • Being inside the raid and ready for buffs at 18.30 grants 50 EP
  • Killing a boss grants 50 EP
  • Staying throughout the entire raid grants 50 EP
  • Being on standby for the raid will grant you as much EP as those in the raid. You'll lose all the EP for that raid if you're called up to step in and are unavailable.
3. Gear Points (GP)
The manner in which you track how "much" gear a single individual has gotten is through Gear Points. The amount of GP a specific item costs is determined by a number of factors, and this is to make sure different items cost different amounts depending on their worth. A 2-handed epic sword should definitively cost more than a rare ring, and an epic ring from a higher tier should cost more than an epic ring from a lower one. The Loot Manager is responsible for the handling of GP, as well as handing out loot. Formulas and costs are available through copying the settings from Zorn in the CEPGP addon.
4. Priority
As stated above, Priority is calculated from EP/GP. When an item drops, interested eligible players will be ranked according to their Priority, with the highest ranking player receiving the item in question. Additionally, PvE main spec is prioritized over off-spec. To put it simply, main spec gear is any gear than you will use in the spec you use for raiding. Off-spec gear is gear that you want to use in PvP or for grinding or whatever else. An example of this would be all 2-handed physical DPS weapons. Since Arms spec is inferior for Alliance warriors, all 2-handed weapons will be considered off-spec. Furthermore, Raiders are prioritized over Trials and Socials. If no Raider wants the item it will go to an off-spec bid. If your rank is Trial or Social, then you will allowed to bid on every off-spec item on equal footing with every else. For Raider ranks, a flat 50% discount (rounding up) is given on all off-spec items, which is applied manually when you get the item. Raiders may not take items for off-spec if they are upgrades for their PvE main-spec.
5. Tanking gear exceptions
As per usual tanks have a priority on all tanking items, and the main tank specifically. This makes sure that the people who have to be hit in the face and survive do just that; survive. But what happens when designated tanks have the loot they need? Then said items will be handed to other Warriors free of charge. Why? Well it is quite simple, without properly geared tanks we will not succeed. When we get far enough they will be required to step up as tanks (looking at you 4 Horsemen). It is unreasonable to expect that DPS warriors should have to spend their EP (which they should be spending on DPS gear) on gear which they may not want or have a need for right now, but that may help the guild in the long run. Thus, left-over pieces of tanking gear will be handed out for free.
6. Special items
The last exception pertains to a specific set of items:
  • Thunderfury will undoubtedly go to the main tank first and to other tanks thereafter. This is because of the insane threat-increase it provides for the tanks, which in turn makes life easier for everyone else.
  • Head of Onyxia. Due to the nature of the rewards available, physical dpsers and tanks will be given priority on this item. Though, since it is a 100% drop and we should be able to kill her 6 times a month, if a caster wants the ring, they should be able to get it in time.
  • Atiesh (all versions). TBD.
  • Black Qiraji Resonating Crystal. TBD.
  • Sulfuras, Hand of Ragnaros. As it has very limited use in PvE, it is considered an off-spec item and will go to any interested eligible player who wishes to spend the resources required to build the hammer.
MC Crafting materials
The guild bank will keep a reserve of these materials on hand. Materials can be purchased by Raider rank members in order to craft a specific main spec item. Materials will be provided to Raiders on a first come first serve-basis provided that the member has procured all other necessary materials for the intended craft (i.e. no hoarding crafting materials if you can't use them to craft an item in a timely manner). We're estimating that we're able to provide materials for 1-3 crafts per week, depending on the requirements of crafted items. Materials are bought with gold at about 50% of the AH value (check the pinned message on Discord in the guild-bank section for pricing) on Sundays before the raid.
7. Decay
Every wednesday, before the start of the raid, everyones' EP and GP values will be reduced by 25%. This is a featured called "Decay". Decay serves two purposes:
  • It prevented raiders with a long and good attendance from amassing so much EP that newer raiders simply cannot compete with them on priority at all, but putting an effective cealing on how much EP a person can have
  • It encourages people to take smaller upgrades while waiting for a rarer item to drop
8. New tiers
When Blackwing Lair comes out, a lot of raiders will be near the EP ceiling. This means a huge advantage for them on the first clears. To ameliorate this, we will be doing a one-time EP decay at the start of the phase that will be equal to the inverse of your attence in Molten Core and Onyxia. That means that if your overall attendance in Phase 1 and 2 was 75%, your EP and GP will be decayed by 25%. If it was 40%, your EP and GP will be decayed by 60%. This is to further award the people with good attendance, without having anyone be too dominant once a new raid rolls out.
9. New members
New members are subject to a trial period spanning 4 raids, after which they may compete with regular raiders for gear.
10. Summary
To bluntly summarize our implementation of EPGP:
  • Do good things, earn EP
  • Get gear, "earn" GP equal to the cost of the item
  • EP divided by GP equals your Priority
  • When an item drops, an eligible member with the highest Priority gets the item
  • Eligibility is determined though class, spec, and guild rank
  • Both EP and GP decay (is reduced) by a fixed percentage at fixed intervals
  • Some restrictions and exceptions apply for specific items
